Sen Game Rules

Preparing the Game

  1. The game is played with 2 players.
  2. The deck consists of four cards of each number from 0 to 8, nine cards with the number 9, and 6 special cards (6P) that allow the player to see the number of any card belonging to either player.
  3. Each player is dealt 4 cards at the beginning of the game. Throughout the game, the players' cards lay face-down on the table.
  4. The rest of the deck makes up the face-down pile.
  5. One card is drawn from the face-down pile and placed on the table face-up; this will be the face-up pile.

Playing the Game

  1. The goal of the game is to accumulate the fewest points by the end of the game.
  2. In their first turn, players get to see two of their cards.
  3. During the game, players take turns in which they can either draw a card from the face-down pile or from the face-up pile.
  4. When a player chooses a card, they can replace one of their cards with it. The replaced card is then placed on the face-up pile.
  5. If the card taken from the face-down pile doesn't suit the player, they can choose to place it on the face-up pile.
  6. If a player draws a 6P card from the face-down pile, they can either use it like a regular card (replace one of their cards with it/put it on the face-up pile) or use it to see the number of any card belonging to either player.
  7. At the beginning of their turn, the player can choose to end the game by clicking the "WAKE UP!" button if they believe they have the least points.

The End of the Game

  1. The game ends when there are no more cards in the face-down pile or when one of the players clicks the "WAKE UP!" button.
  2. If a player has the highest number of 9 cards, they are not counted in their final score. If both players have the same number of 9 cards, however, they count towards their score.
  3. The player with the lower score wins.
